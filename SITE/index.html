001 — About

Four Voices.
One Conversation.

Art+OBJECTS presents a curated selection of contemporary art and design at Tbilisi Art Fair 2026. Uniting painting, sculpture, ceramics, and functional art, the booth explores the boundary where fine art meets the tangible world.

From Dubinsky's narrative oil stick canvases capturing post-Soviet nostalgia and Tbilisi's everyday poetry, to Lizi Budagashvili's ethereal dreamscapes woven from childhood memory — from Daria Krotova's haunting ceramic faces to SKNYPL's sculptural furniture hewn from 200-year-old Georgian pine — each artist transforms the familiar into the extraordinary.

This is art you can live with. Objects you can live in.

Dubinsky_

003 — Artist

Lizi
Budagashvili

Born in Tbilisi (b. 2000), currently based in New York. MFA from the New York Academy of Art, BFA from Ringling College. Two-time recipient of the Elizabeth Greenshields Foundation Grant. Her paintings capture the longing for simpler times, delving into ephemeral, mirage-like childhood recollections through transparent veils and dreamlike atmospheres.

004 — Artist

Daria
Krotova

Sculptor and multimedia artist working across ceramics and works on paper. Krotova's ceramic faces — expressive, vulnerable, haunting — hover between portraiture and emotional archaeology. Her abstract paper works layer graphic marks with bursts of pure color, creating constellations of contained energy.

005 — Artist / Design

SKNYPL

SKNYPL repurposes 200-year-old pine from a Georgian railway sawmill, cutting and reassembling the wood into modular sculptural furniture. Each piece carries the weight of industrial history — transformed into living objects that blur the line between sculpture and function, heritage and future.
